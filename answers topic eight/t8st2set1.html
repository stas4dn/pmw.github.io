SubTopic 2: Internet Services

Qn3. State and explain any 5(five) internet services

  • Telnet – enables users to use the resources of a computer in another part of the world
  • Email – It allows the transfer of messages, documents, and pictures among others, across the Internet
  • Mailing list: a list of the names and addresses of people to whom advertising matter, information, or other material may be mailed regularly
  • Internet Relay Chat (IRC) – A live interactive discussion in which parties on the network exchange ideas and observation electronically.
  • File Transfer Protocol – The standard method for transferring files, whether downloading or uploading, to and from your computer with another computer on the Internet
  • Newsgroups – Worldwide discussion areas where notices can be posted for anyone to view
  • World Wide Web – This refers to the global collection of websites consisting of linked electronic documents called Web pages stored on internet servers all over the world
  • Instant Messaging: A combination of real-time chat and e-mail by which short text messages are rapidly exchanged over the Internet, with messages appearing on recipient’s display screen immediately upon arrival
  • Usenet: A system of worldwide discussion groups
  • Internet Telephony or Voice over IP: Real-time voice conversations transmitted between computers on the Internet
  • Web Directory: A listing of Web sites and their URLs, categorized by topic
  • Electronic Commerce/e-commerce: Conducting commercial activities on the Internet
  • Social Networking. A type of service where users can seek others who share their interests, find out what's going on in their areas of concern, and share information with one another (e.g. Facebook, Twitter).
  • Cloud Computing: A service in which computer software, hardware and data are used remotely over the Internet, instead of acquiring and using them on a local computer.
  • Search Engines: Software programs that look through the Web to locate sites matching a keyword entered by the user NB: Keyword: A string of letters or words that indicates the subject to be searched.

Qn4. Define the term electronic mail

Messages distributed by electronic means from one computer user to one or more recipients via a network

Qn7. Differentiate between email and email software

An email refers to Messages distributed by electronic means from one computer user to one or more recipients via a network WHILE An email software also called An email client, email reader or, more formally, message user agent or mail user agent is a computer program used to access and manage a user's email.

Qn8. Name the different parts of an email address (stasfoundation@gmail.com)

  • The username is the first part of an email address
  • The @ symbol: It means that the username belongs to the domain on the other side of the @ symbol
  • Mail Server: It indicates the organization that hosts the server where their emails go
  • Top-Level Domain: The top-level domain tells users what type of organization they are emailing
