Qn32. Why is a projector necessary in a computer laboratory?

A data projector takes the image that displays on a computer screen and projects it onto a large screen or wall so that an audience of people can see the image clearly

Qn33. Define a printer

A printer is a device that produces a hard copy output such as text and graphics on a physical material like paper.

Qn34. The speed of a printer is measured in?

Today's modern digital printers (laser and inkjet) are measured in “pages per minute” (ppm). high-speed newspaper printers (offset and gravure) where they use continue form of paper reel are measured in “Feet per minute” (fpm). Print speed is typically measured in pages per minute (ppm).

Qn35. Briefly explain the two categories of printers

a. Impact printers

An impact printer forms characters and graphics on a piece of paper by a striking mechanism against an ink ribbon that physically contacts the paper

b. Nonimpact printers

A nonimpact printer forms characters and graphics on a piece of paper without actually striking the paper
