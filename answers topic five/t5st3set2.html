Qn55. What is meant by;

  1. Sequential access

    2. data must be accessed in the order in which it is stored

  2. Random access

    3. Random Access is the ability to have access to any item from a given population of elements at random.

  3. Sector

    4. A division of a storage medium on a hard drive or diskette that is a wedge-shaped section of one of the circular tracks OR A Sector is the intersection between a mathematical sector and a track

  4. Track

    5. A disk drive track is a circular path on the surface of a disk or diskette on which information is magnetically recorded and from which recorded information is read.

  5. Head crash

    6. A head crash is a hard-disk failure that occurs when a readâ€“write head of a hard disk drive makes contact with its rotating platter, slashing its surface and permanently damaging its magnetic media

  6. Backup

    7. A backup is a duplicate of a file, program, or disk placed on a separate storage medium that you can use in case the original is lost, damaged, or destroyed

  7. Capacity

    8. The amount of data, instructions and/or information a storage medium can accommodate

  8. Access time

    9. The time a program or device takes to locate a single piece of information and make it available to the computer for processing.

  9. Volatile memory

    10. This is a type of memory that loses its content when power is switched off
