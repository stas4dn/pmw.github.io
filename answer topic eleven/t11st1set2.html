Qn7. Define data transmission media

The term transmission media refers to any physical or non-physical link / pathway between two or more devices and in which a signal can be made to flow from source to destination

Qn8. Distinguish between guided/physical and unguided/wireless transmission media

Physical media transmits data signals from the source to the destination through a restricted pathway such as a cable WHILE Wireless or unbounded media is that is where data signals flow through the air.

Qn11. Explain the following examples of guided transmission media

a. Twisted pair cable

Twisted pair cable is made up of solid copper wire strands wound in pairs within a single media commonly used to interconnect devices on a Local Area Network

b. Coaxial cable

Coaxial cables consist of a core copper wire in the center enclosed in an insulating sheath, followed by hollow mesh conductor which is covered by a shield making the cable more resistant to electromagnetic interference than the twisted pair cable

c. Fibre optic cable

Fibre optic cable utilizes light to transmit data from one point to another on the network.
